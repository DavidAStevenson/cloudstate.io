Why?

Serverless computing makes developers more productive by taking care of operational concerns for them.

However, serverless functions fail to address the challenges associated with managing state.

CloudState sits at the confluence between serverless and state, making developers more productive by handling the operational and distribution concerns of state for them.

What?

CloudState provides an architectural approach and associated protocol for providing common distributed state management patterns. There are no limits on which patterns can be supported, however current and envisioned supported patterns include:

  • Event Sourcing
  • CRDTs
  • P2P messaging
  • CQRS read side projections

A sidecar is provided that handles all incoming communication, as well as communiaction to other services and databases. The sidecar is also responsible for concerns such as clustering, sharding, P2P messaging, and replication.

The protocol allows the multiplexing of state and incoming communication on the same channel, allowing developers to focus only on their business logic, and not how state is managed, distributed, and stored.

The sidecars have an intimate understanding of the state management patterns being used, and consequently are able to apply intelligent autoscaling based on that knowledge, and are able to give in depth observabiility into exactly what is happening.

CloudState can be used either by itself, in combination with a service mesh, or it is in envisioned that it will integrated with other serverless technologies such as Knative.
